However, Angola is one of the poorest countries in Africa, and the number of people with access to either a television or radio is comparatively few.

"Now things are a little calmer, there are slightly less people - but, after two and a half years of broadcasting, there are still a lot of people going."

Angolan families who are attempting to track each other down, after being separated by nearly 30 years of war, are succeeding thanks to a hugely popular TV show.

Every Friday, hundreds of people gather in Luanda's Independence Square to record a message in front of the TV cameras, in the hope that a lost relative will see it.

The media in the southern African country, twice the size of France, has been gripped by the quest of so many people to find their relatives.

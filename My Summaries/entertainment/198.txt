She played Nellie in Mike Leigh's latest film Vera Drake, which won the Golden Lion prize for best film at this year's Venice Film Festival.

Other TV appearances included the part of Matron in ITV drama Footballers' Wives and a cameo role as Tom Farrell's mother Sheila in BBC comedy Gimme Gimme Gimme.

Born in Runcorn, Cheshire, she also starred in numerous films including 1989's Shirley Valentine.

Keaveney's career also included stage performances in Neaptide for the National Theatre, Private Lives and The Rise and Fall of Little Voice.

Keaveney, who played Brookside's Marie Jackson when the Channel 4 soap began in 1982, died on Saturday.

Shameless, State Of Play and Clocking Off creator Paul Abbott got a lifetime achievement award while former Grange Hill actress Amma Asante won the breakthrough award for writing and directing her first film, A Way Of Life.

Little Britain was named best comedy while Franz Ferdinand beat Morrissey and The Libertines to the music award.

Author David Mitchell made up for losing out in the Booker Prize to Alan Hollinghurst by beating him to the literary award with his book Cloud Atlas.

In other South Bank categories, Little Britain's second series beat Nighty Night and The Green Wing to the comedy trophy while Alan Bennett's The History Boys won in the theatre category.

In 2004, Coronation Street pulled away from its BBC One rival EastEnders in the ratings and dominated other TV awards.

Actress Patsy Rowlands, known to millions for her roles in the Carry On films, has died at the age of 71.

In recent years she appeared in BBC mini-series The Cazalets and played Mrs Potts in the London stage version of Beauty and the Beast.

Rowlands starred in nine of the popular Carry On films, alongside fellow regulars Sid James, Kenneth Williams and Barbara Windsor.

"She was of the old school - she had skills from musical theatre and high drama, that is why she worked with the great and the good of directors.

She made her first Carry On film in 1969 where she appeared in Carry On Again Doctor.

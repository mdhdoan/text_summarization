Looking ahead, the group is anticipating a quiet start to 2005 with the market picking up in the second half - prompting a 3% rise in prices over the coming 12 months.

The figures support recent data from the government and other bodies which all point to a slowdown in the housing market.

A total of 48% of chartered surveyor estate agents reported lower prices in the three months to November - the highest level in 12 years.

The figures were published as another survey by property website Rightmove said the average asking price of a home fell by more than £600 from £190,329 in November to £189,733 in December.

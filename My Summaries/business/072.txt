Mr Park's statement came as the bank decided to keep interest rates at an all-time low of 3.25%.

South Korea looks set to sustain its revival thanks to renewed private consumption, its central bank says.

Economic growth in 2004 was about 4.7%, with the central bank predicting 4% growth this year.

Exports - traditionally the driver for expansion in Asian economies - grew slower in January than at any time in 17 months.

The country's economy has suffered from an overhang of personal debt after its consumers' credit card spending spree.

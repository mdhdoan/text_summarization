The latest to pitch in has been President George W Bush himself, who told the Asia Pacific Economic Co-operation (Apec) summit in Chile that he remained committed to halving the budget deficit.

Rising oil prices and the sinking dollar hit shares on Monday after a finance ministers' meeting and stern words from Fed chief Alan Greenspan.

In London, the benchmark Brent crude price closed down 51 cents at $44.38 a barrel, while New York light sweet crude closed down 25 cents at $48.64 a barrel.

G20 finance ministers said nothing about supporting the dollar, whose slide could further jeopardise growth in Japan and Europe.

And last week US Treasury Secretary John Snow told an audience in the UK that the policy remained unaltered.

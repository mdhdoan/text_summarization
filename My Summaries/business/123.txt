Metrovias, the private firm which runs the five tube lines in the city, has offered an 8% increase in wages.

Underground staff have warned they will continue with the protests until the management put an acceptable offer on the table.

Tube workers walked out last week demanding a 53% pay rise and in protest against the installation of automatic ticket machines.

The Argentine Work Ministry has been mediating in the conflict and it could call an "obligatory conciliation", which would force both sides to find a solution and put an end to the conflict.

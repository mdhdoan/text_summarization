China's economy is not only gathering speed thanks to domestic demand, but also from soaring sales overseas.

Growth in industrial output - the main target of government efforts to impose curbs on credit and investments - was 11.5% in 2004, down from 17% the previous year.

China raised rates by 0.27 percentage points to 5.8% - its first hike in nine years - in October 2004.

And overall investment in fixed assets was still high, up 21.3% from the previous year - although some way off the peak of 43% seen in the first quarter of 2004.

Delta, which lost $646m in the three months to September, was forced to cut 6,900 jobs worldwide as part of its aim to slash $5bn from its costs.

Other US carriers, including United, have sought bankruptcy protection, amid high fuel costs and competition from discount carriers.

In October, it reached a crucial agreement with pilots on pay and conditions and it has also issued new shares to staff in return for wage cuts.

This latest move to boost business has prompted speculation other firms will be forced to match their fares, hurting revenues in the sector.

Airline shares closed lower on the announcement, with Delta, Continental and American Airlines all falling by more than 7%.

France's economic growth accelerated in the last three months of 2004, driven by consumer spending, a report shows.

"In the beginning of 2004, growth was mainly driven by consumer spending.

Consumer spending was up by 1.2% in the fourth quarter, and there also was a rebound in business investment that gave the recovery an extra shove.

"Consumer spending is still high.

As well as strong domestic demand, exports climbed by 1.3% in the fourth quarter - the biggest increase in foreign sales for a year.

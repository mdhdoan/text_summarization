"In our business the market is absolutely global, you can not imagine living with our size (of business) even within an enlarged European Union, (if we did that) we would need not 350 people but perhaps just 150 or 200," he says.

In Germany, industry is exporting more these days, but the economy as a whole is once again mired in slow growth and high unemployment.

Director Hugues Charbonnier says he is under pressure because factories in the Far East are able to make some of his output more cheaply, while his key markets are now in China and India.

The EU set itself the target of being the most efficient economy in the world by 2010.

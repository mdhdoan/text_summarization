In 2002, the KSE was the world's best performing stock market, with the index rising 112%.

Energy and telecommunication stocks performed particularly well, recording an 8%-10% rise since Monday morning.

The market is abuzz with speculation that substantial investment may now start to flow in from the US, a country seen locally as deeply interested in defusing tensions between the South Asian neighbours.

"Coupled with the 7% GDP growth expected by June this year, I am least surprised at the market's performance," says Mr Lakhani.

"If you file a $1.3 trillion case against Saudi money after 9/11, Arab money will not go to the US any more."

Sales in what is traditionally a strong month rose by 0.5% on a like-for-like basis, compared with a year earlier.The previous BRC survey found Christmas 2004 was the worst for 10 years for retailers.In the three-months to January, like-for-like sales showed a growth rate of -0.1%, the same as in the three months to December, the BRC said.But clothing and footwear sales were said to be generally better than December, while department stores also had a good month.And according to Office for National Statistics data, sales in December failed to meet expectations and by some counts were the worst since 1981.

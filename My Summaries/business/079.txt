Almost a quarter of a million US consumers complained of being targeted for identity theft in 2004, official figures suggest.

ID theft surge hits US consumers

Another 18% came from attempts to rip off people's bank accounts, while 13% of cases concerned attempts to defraud employers by abusing someone else's identity.

The report marks the fifth year in a row in which identity fraud has topped the table.

Catalogue and home-shopping frauds were next in line, accounting for 8% of total complaints, while concerns about internet services and computers - including spyware found on people's PCs and undisclosed charges for websites - amounted to 6% of complaints.

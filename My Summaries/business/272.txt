Brazil's currency, the real, also rose to its highest level against the dollar in more than two years on Friday.

The Brazilian stock market has risen to a record high as investors display growing confidence in the durability of the country's economic recovery.

"For the first time in decades, we have all three economic policy pillars in line during a recovery," Finance Minister Antonio Palocci told the Associated Press news agency.

"There should be more space for gains until the end of the year, somewhere up to 27,000 points," said Paschoal Tadeu Buonomo, head of equities trading at brokers TOV.

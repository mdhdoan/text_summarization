Although interest rates still stand at a punitive 17.25%, inflation has fallen from 9% to 7% while exports are booming, particularly of agricultural products.

The Brazilian stock market has risen to a record high as investors display growing confidence in the durability of the country's economic recovery.

Analysts believe the stock market will rise above the 25,000 mark for the first time before too long.

"For the first time in decades, we have all three economic policy pillars in line during a recovery," Finance Minister Antonio Palocci told the Associated Press news agency.

Investors have praised his handling of the economy as foreign investment has risen, unemployment has fallen and inflation has been brought under control.

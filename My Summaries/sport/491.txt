Spain's Rafael Nadal beats Andy Roddick of the USA in the second singles match rubber of the 2004 Davis Cup final in Seville.

Three times, Nadal forces a break point and three times the world number two hangs in.

Both players come up with some scintillating tennis and the Spaniard has several chances to clinch the game before finally doing so when Roddick drives wide.

Roddick saves the break point and then bangs down his ninth ace before clinching the game with a service winner.

Nadal works Roddick around the court on two consecutive points to earn two break points.
